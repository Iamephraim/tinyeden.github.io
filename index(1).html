Current focus

Build something elegant, but not loud.

The design language stays modern and expressive, with enough atmosphere to feel premium without turning into a visual overload.

Material reality

Accessible food-safe materials, reasonable cost, durability, and easy cleaning at home.

Control simplicity

Temperature, pH, dissolved oxygen, agitation — only as much control as the use case truly needs.

Human fit

Ergonomics, aesthetics, and a minimum-skill operating experience that belongs on a countertop.

Direction

Where the idea is going

Now

Prove feasibility. Keep the scope disciplined. Verify that the device can be built, cleaned, and operated simply.

Next

Refine the form factor, volume, and control stack so the device feels natural in a kitchen setting.

Later

Only after feasibility comes deeper iteration. Commercialization remains outside the current scope.
